ENTEROBIOME KIDS - Microbiome Development

Four Phases of Gut Microbiota Formation in Children

Birth

Streptococcus spp., Enterobacterales

14 Days

Bifidobacterium, Lactobacillaceae, Clostridium

Complementary Foods

Bacteroidota, Bacillota, Verrucomicrobiota

Weaning

Stabilization of microbiota

Factors Affecting Microbiota Formation

  • Type of birth: Naturally born infants have stable composition; C-section infants show delayed colonization and more opportunistic microorganisms
  • Feeding type: Breastfed infants dominated by Bifidobacterium spp.; formula-fed dominated by Bacteroides and Clostridium
  • Gestational age: Preterm infants show delayed anaerobic colonization with higher Enterobacteriaceae
  • Antibiotic use: Reduces diversity, increases drug-resistant bacteria, decreases Bifidobacteriaceae

A species-rich gut ecosystem is more resilient to adverse environmental stresses. Reduced diversity is associated with disease presence.
