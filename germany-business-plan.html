1. Executive Summary

Genomer Biotechnology is a Türkiye-based in vitro diagnostic (IVD) company that develops and manufactures real-time PCR (qPCR) kits in its ISO 13485-certified production facility. The company is currently undergoing IVDR 2017/746 conformity assessment for a defined portfolio of Class B and Class C kits, with a Notified Body engagement already initiated.

In preparation for entry into the European Union market, Genomer will establish a liaison office (Verbindungsbüro) in Berlin, Germany. The liaison office will not engage in commercial activity. Its objectives are limited to scientific relationship building with Key Opinion Leaders (KOLs), market intelligence gathering, promotional groundwork and on-the-ground coordination of the IVDR certification process. Once IVDR certification is in place, the liaison office will be converted into a GmbH with full commercial capacity (Phase 2).

Phase 1 — Now
Liaison office in Berlin. Promotion, KOL engagement, market research, IVDR coordination. No revenue.
Phase 2 — ~12 months
Conversion to GmbH following IVDR certification. Full commercial activity in Germany and EU.
Funding
100% funded from the parent company's own resources. No external financing required for Phase 1.
Personnel

4. Strategic Rationale — Why Germany, Why Now

4.1 Germany as the EU Entry Point

Germany is the largest healthcare market in the European Union and one of the most regulated. Achieving credibility in Germany is widely regarded as a prerequisite for sustainable expansion into other EU markets. The country also hosts a dense network of university hospitals, reference laboratories and Key Opinion Leaders in women's health, urology and gastroenterology — the precise clinical disciplines addressed by Genomer's portfolio.

4.2 Berlin as the Office Location

Berlin offers immediate proximity to Charité — Universitätsmedizin Berlin, one of Europe's leading academic medical centres, as well as easy access to Munich (Technische Universität München) and Heidelberg (Universität Heidelberg) — the three KOL clusters most relevant to Genomer's portfolio. Berlin also provides a cost-efficient and internationally connected operating base for a small representative office.

4.3 Timing — Aligned with IVDR Certification

The IVDR 2017/746 transition has fundamentally reshaped the European IVD landscape, creating a window of opportunity for new entrants who arrive with a fully certified, IVDR-native portfolio. Establishing the liaison office now — during the certification period — enables Genomer to build scientific awareness and customer pipelines in parallel with the regulatory process, so that commercial conversion (Phase 2) can begin as soon as certification is granted.

5. Phase 1 — Liaison Office Operations

5.1 Scope

The Berlin liaison office will operate as a non-trading representative office under German law (Verbindungsbüro). It will not place products on the EU market, will not invoice customers and will not generate revenue. Its activities are scientific, promotional and preparatory in nature.

5.2 Key Activities

  • KOL engagement — structured outreach to clinicians and researchers at Charité, TU München and Universität Heidelberg (and adjacent centres) covering gynaecology, urology, paediatric gastroenterology and clinical microbiology.
  • Scientific events — representation at key German and EU congresses (e.g. DGGG, MEDICA, ECCMID, EAU) for awareness and lead generation, with formal product promotion contingent on IVDR certification.
  • Market research — mapping of laboratory chains, reimbursement frameworks (GKV / GOÄ), tendering practices and competitive positioning.
  • Distributor pre-screening — identification and qualification of potential distribution partners for Phase 2.
  • IVDR coordination — local interface with the Notified Body, regulatory consultants and clinical evaluation experts.

5.3 Operating Model

Phase 1 is a deliberately lean, low-overhead structure. No local employees will be hired. All activities will be carried out by Genomer's Türkiye-based management and scientific team through periodic visits to Germany combined with structured digital coordination. The Berlin office will provide a registered address and on-demand meeting infrastructure.

6. Phase 2 — Conversion into a GmbH

Following completion of IVDR certification (currently anticipated within approximately 12 months), the liaison office will be converted into a GmbH. The GmbH will assume full commercial capacity — product distribution, sales, customer invoicing, distributor management and, where appropriate, the role of EU Authorised Representative for Genomer's products under IVDR Article 11. Conversion will be staged so that commercial activity begins only after all regulatory prerequisites have been satisfied.

9. Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risk Mitigation
IVDR certification delay Phase 1 activities (KOL engagement, market research) are non-commercial and do not depend on certification timing. Phase 2 conversion is sequenced to follow certification, not predate it.
Currency / cost variation Conservative budget ranges, 10% contingency line, and quarterly review against actuals.
Regulatory misclassification Liaison office structured strictly as Verbindungsbüro under German law; legal counsel engaged to verify boundary conditions before Phase 2.
Reputation / unauthorised promotion All product-level promotional content gated until IVDR certification is granted; pre-certification activities limited to scientific awareness and pipeline preparation.

10. Conclusion

The establishment of a Berlin liaison office is a measured, low-risk and regulation-aligned step that prepares Genomer's entry into the European diagnostic market. Phase 1 builds the scientific credibility, customer pipeline and regulatory presence necessary for a successful Phase 2 commercial launch through a German GmbH. The entire programme is funded from the parent company's resources, requires no external financing, and is governed by strict scope boundaries to ensure full compliance with German and European Union legislation throughout.
