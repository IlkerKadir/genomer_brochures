ENTEROBIOME® Kids

ENTEROBIOME® Kids

Reagent kit for the analysis of gut microbiota composition in stool samples from children by real-time PCR

The human gut microbiota is a complex, dynamic ecosystem inhabited by a huge number of different microorganisms, including bacteria, fungi, archaea, and viruses. They interact with each other and with the human organism, participating in a variety of metabolic processes.

The gut microbiota is involved in carbohydrate and protein metabolism, lipid exchange, and also produces important metabolic intermediates such as short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), secondary bile acids (BAs), vitamins and lipopolysaccharides. These metabolites act as signaling molecules and are involved in the functioning of all systems of the human organism.

Childhood is a period of gut microbiome formation

The establishment of the infant's own gut microbiota is a long and dynamic process in which four successive phases are distinguished. The composition and diversity of the gut microbiota change significantly during childhood, influenced by delivery mode, feeding type, diet, antibiotic use, and gestational age.

Genomer HPV Screen

Qualitative Real-Time PCR • 14 High-Risk Types • 96 Reactions/Kit

Qualitative DNA detection of 14 types of human papillomavirus (HPV) with high carcinogenic risk associated with cervical cancer. Provides differential (individual) detection of HPV types 16, 18, and 45; the remaining 11 types are detected as a pooled result.

HPV Types Detected

Individually genotyped HPV 16, HPV 18, HPV 45
Pooled group detection HPV 31, 33, 35, 39, 51, 52, 56, 58, 59, 66, 68

Clinical Indications

  • •Dynamic monitoring of HPV-associated diseases

Genomer HPV Quant 15

Genomer HPV Quant 15

Quantitative Real-Time PCR • 15 Types (13 HR + 2 LR) • 48 Reactions/Kit

In vitro DNA test for specific identification and quantification of 15 HPV genotypes. Provides both absolute quantification (log copies/sample) and relative quantification normalized to 105 human cells for viral load monitoring.

HPV Types Detected

High-risk group 1 HPV 16, 31, 33, 35, 52, 58 (detected as group)
High-risk group 2 HPV 18, 39, 45, 59 (detected as group)
Individually identified HR HPV 51, HPV 56, HPV 68
Low-risk (grouped) HPV 6, HPV 11

Quantification Method

Absolute quantification Viral load as log (copies/sample) — common logarithm of HPV DNA copy number per sample
Relative quantification HPV DNA normalized to human cell count (per 105 cells) — biomarker for viral replication activity
Sample Intake Control SIC estimates human DNA amount; SIC < 4.0 indicates insufficient sampling (repeat required)

Technical Specifications

Method Quantitative real-time PCR (fluorescent TaqMan probes)
Reactions per kit 48
Tube format 0.2 mL × 8-tube PCR strips
Analytical sensitivity 5 copies per amplification tube
Sample types Epithelial cell swabs, prostate fluid, ejaculate, urine, biopsy
Detection channels FAM, HEX (IC), ROX, Cy5
